Ball Position & Tips
Ball Position — Top View (Lefty)
  • Hands slightly ahead of the ball — shaft leans toward target at address
  • Weight 50/50 at setup, shifts fully to lead side at impact
  • These clubs build your core contact habit — quality over distance
  • Watch your divot: it should appear to the RIGHT of where the ball was
Gap Wedge (50°): Your new scoring club. Same swing as the PW — ball center of stance, hands forward, trust the loft. Focus on consistent distance control at 70–90 yards. Don't try to max it out — find your reliable carry number.
⚠ Common Mistake

Trying to scoop the ball airborne. Trust the loft — hit down and through, not up. The club's loft does the lifting.

Ball Position — Top View (Lefty)

Swinging harder with longer clubs. Swing wider and smoother — the extra shaft length adds distance automatically.

Ball Position & Tee Setup
Ball Position — Top View (Lefty)
Tee Setup — 2¾" with 3 Marks
  • Driver is the ONLY club you hit on the upswing — ball forward enables this
  • Widen stance to shoulder-width or beyond for stability
  • Tilt spine slightly away from target — weight a touch trail at address
  • Smoother tempo = straighter, longer drives. Harder = more curve, less control
⚠ Common Mistake

Ball too far back in the stance — forces a downward hit on the driver, killing distance. Ball off lead heel is the rule.

🏁 Phase 4 — Short Game Finish
Chipping
Bump & Run — 5 chips 🎯
7-Iron, PW, or GW (50°)10–25 yardsPick a landing spot
Drop 5 balls just off the edge of the green. Pick a landing spot 1–2 feet onto the green and aim for that — not the hole. Let the ball run the rest. The bump-and-run is your most consistent scoring shot as a lefty.
💬 Weight forward, hands ahead, brush the grass. It's a long putting stroke with a lofted club.
🎯 Gap Wedge (50°): Excellent for bump and run from 15–25 yards — more loft than the PW, lands softer with less run. Great option when you need to carry a small amount of rough before the green.
Technique
Setup: Ball back in stance (toward trail foot). Weight 70% on lead foot. Hands pressed forward — shaft leans toward target. This delofts the club and keeps trajectory low.
Stroke: Pendulum motion — no wrist flip. Think of it as a long putt. The club brushes the turf, doesn't dig. Follow through matches your backswing length.
⚠ Watch For

Scooping — trying to lift the ball. Trust the loft. Hit down and through. The divot should be small and in front of where the ball was.

Putting
3-6-9 Putting Challenge 📏
Putter10 puttsPractice green
Place 3 balls at 3ft, 3 at 6ft, 3 at 9ft, and 1 at your max comfortable distance. Goal: make the 3-footers, 2-putt the rest. Count your score — 10 makeable putts.
💬 Eyes over the ball. Pendulum. Listen for the rattle — don't watch it.
Drills
Gate drill: Push two tees into the green slightly wider than your putter face, 6 inches in front of the ball. Roll the ball through the gate. Miss the tees = good path. Simple and brutal.
Distance control drill: Forget the hole. Putt 5 balls to the fringe trying to stop them within 1 foot of the edge. No hole, just speed. This is where 3-putts are won and lost.
Golden Rules
Always aim at a specific target — never hit to nowhere.
One swing thought per session. Never change mid-session.
Bad shot? Reset, breathe, commit to the next one.
Sand Wedge — chipping green only for now.
Pro lesson at your local course: 4–6 weeks in.

Pro Tip: Close your eyes for one of the practice swings and just feel the rotation. Where does the club feel heaviest? That's the club head doing its job.
Phase 1
Contact Challenge 🎯
9-Iron or 7-Iron~10 balls
The Game: Hit 7 out of 10 in the air. Count every airborne shot out loud. Celebrate every one that flies! Focus on making contact — not how far it goes.
💬 Feel: Hit DOWN on it — the club does the lifting!
More Detail
Ball position: middle of your stance. Feet shoulder-width. Weight 50/50. Take a normal swing — don't try to scoop the ball up. The loft on the 9-iron does that automatically.
The Game Goal: If you hit 7+ in the air = you WIN Phase 1! Move to Phase 2. Under 7? No big deal — take 5 more tries. Every shot is practice.
⚠ Watch For

Trying to help the ball up by scooping. Hit through the ball into the ground. The divot (or brushed grass) should be in FRONT of where the ball was.

Phase 2
Distance Explorer 📏
7-IronFW Wood 23°~15 balls
The Game: Try different clubs and see which goes farthest. Hit 5 with 7-iron, 5 with the Fairway Wood, and 5 mixing it up. Remember your personal record!
💬 Feel: Longer club = wider, smoother swing. Same sequence!
More Detail
Ball position moves forward as clubs get longer — for the fairway wood, play it just inside your LEFT heel (lead heel for a right-hander). Same swing sequence — just a wider arc.
Track your distances: Where does your 7-iron land on average? What about the FW Wood? This is super useful info — remember it!
Phase 3
Rocket Launch 🚀 — Driver Only!
Driver 17°~5 ballsMax fun
Just rip it. Tee it up, swing as hard as you want, and watch it go. No swing thoughts — just pure fun. If it goes sideways, laugh and try again.
💬 Feel: Sweep it up off the tee. Smooth is fast!
Tee Setup
Ball position: off your LEFT (lead) heel — as far forward as it goes. Tee height: about half the ball above the club face. Wide stance. Weight slightly back.
Driver is the ONE club you hit on the upswing — ball position forward makes this happen automatically. Just trust the setup and swing!
🏁 Phase 4 — Putting Game
Putting
Ring of Fire 🔥
Putter5 ballsPractice green
Place 5 balls in a circle around the hole, all exactly 3 feet away. You have to make ALL 5 in a row. Miss one? Start over. This is the same drill tour pros use every day.
💬 Pick a spot on the back of the cup and aim for that. Don't aim at the middle — aim for a specific point.
How to Make Every 3-Footer
🎯 Eyes Over the Ball
Stand over the ball and drop another ball from your nose — it should land on your ball. If it lands in front, you're too far away. If behind, too close. Eyes directly over ball = consistent path.
🎯 Pendulum Only
Shoulders rock, wrists stay locked. Imagine your arms and putter are one rigid triangle. Backswing and follow-through are the same length. No flipping the wrists at impact.
Kid's Golden Rules 🌟
Count your GOOD shots — forget the bad ones immediately.
Water break whenever you want. Seriously.
Stop when it stops being fun. Quality over quantity.
⏱ Keep sessions to 20–30 minutes max — short and focused beats long and tired.
One swing thought only: "TURN and FIRE!"

Range Session
Smooth & Easy
20-ball plan · tempo over power · let the club do the work
Before You Hit 🌸
Warm-Up First
Take 2–3 minutes to gently loosen up before reaching for a club. A few slow shoulder rolls, gentle hip circles, and easy torso turns are all you need. There's no rush — the range will wait for you.
"A warm body swings a happy club."
Phase 1 · Feel the Swing (6 balls)
7-Wood or Hybrid
Your most forgiving clubs. Start here every single time. No full swings yet — just easy half-swings to reconnect with the motion. Focus on the rhythm of the club moving back and through. If it feels good after 3, take a fuller swing on the last 3.
Half swing first Smooth tempo No power needed
Feel the clubhead swinging — let gravity do the work on the way down.
Phase 2 · Easy Irons (8 balls)
7-Iron or 8-Iron
Hit 4 balls with the 7-iron, then 4 with the 8-iron. These hollow-body irons are very forgiving — even off-center hits will fly nicely. One swing thought only: finish with your belt buckle facing the target.
Light grip Smooth finish Watch it fly 🌸
Swing to a full finish. If you finish tall, the swing was good.
Phase 3 · Short Game Finish (6 balls)
Pitching Wedge
End every session with short, gentle chip shots. Pick a spot about 20–30 yards away. Small, controlled swings — just enough to get the ball there softly. This is the most relaxing part of the session and builds great feel.
Tiny swing Land softly Breathe easy
"Small swing, big confidence." End on a great feeling.
Grandma's Swing Keys 💜
1
Light Grip 🤲
  • Hold the club gently — tension is the enemy of a smooth swing
  • Imagine holding a tube of toothpaste without squeezing any out
2
Slow Back, Smooth Through
  • Take the club back slowly — there's no hurry
  • Let the swing flow through naturally
3
Full Finish
  • Swing all the way to a tall, balanced finish
  • If you can hold the finish for 2 seconds, the swing was perfect
4
Watch the Ball Fly 🌸
  • Keep your eyes on the ball through contact
  • Then enjoy watching it soar — you earned it
Optional · Driver Day 🚀
When You're Feeling Good
On days when you feel loose and energized, grab the driver and have some fun. No pressure — this is purely for enjoyment. The driver is the longest club in the bag, so it rewards a smooth, easy swing more than anything else.
3–5 balls only Same easy tempo Feel, don't force
🏌️ Tee it up high — half the ball above the clubface
🌿 Stand tall, grip light — the driver does the work with a smooth swing
🎵 Slow backswing — take it back nice and wide, no rush at all
💜 Sweep, don't hit — think of brushing the tee away, not smashing the ball
🌸 Hold that finish — tall and balanced, watch it fly
Skip this section any day you're not feeling it — the 7-Wood is always just as satisfying. 💜

Gentle Warm-Up
Move With Ease
5 min · take breaks as needed · your pace, your way
Before You Begin
All of these movements are gentle and optional. Do only what feels comfortable, and take a breath or short break between exercises whenever you need to. There is no wrong way to warm up — any movement is a win.
Shoulder & Neck
🌀 Slow Shoulder Rolls
Roll both shoulders forward 5 times, then backward 5 times. Slow and smooth. Feel the tension gently melt away.
Let gravity help — no need to force the movement.
↔️ Gentle Neck Turns
Slowly turn your head left, then right. Hold each side for 3 slow breaths. Just a gentle rotation — no strain needed.
Torso & Spine
🌿 Standing Torso Twist
Cross your arms over your chest. Gently rotate your upper body to the right, then to the left. Move only as far as feels comfortable — pause and breathe whenever you need to. This is the core of your golf swing motion.
This is your golf swing! Every gentle twist makes it feel more natural on the course.
🏌️ Slow Practice Swings
Without a ball, make 5 very slow, smooth practice swings with your 7-Wood or a mid-iron. Focus on how the swing feels — not how far the ball would go. This wakes up the right muscles gently.
Hands & Wrists
🤲 Gentle Wrist Circles
Hold your arms in front of you and make slow circles with your wrists — 5 times each direction. Keeps the wrists loose and your grip relaxed.
🤝 Finger Flex & Release
Open your hands wide, then gently close them. Repeat 5 times. A simple way to warm up your grip before holding a club.
Hips & Balance
🔄 Gentle Hip Circles
Stand with feet shoulder-width apart. Make slow, gentle circles with your hips — 5 each direction. Rest between sets if needed. This helps free up the lower body for a smooth turn.
Hold a club or chair for balance if you'd like — take your time with every movement.

The Most Reliable Chip in the Bag
Ball back in stance 70% weight lead Hands forward
Ball positioned toward your trail foot. Weight 70% on lead (right) foot at setup and through impact. Shaft leans toward target — hands ahead of the ball. This delofts the club and keeps the trajectory low and rolling.
💬 Pendulum stroke — no wrist flip. Think long putt with a lofted club. Follow-through matches backswing length.
Club Selection Guide
🏒 7-Iron — Lots of run. Best from 5+ yards off the fringe on flat ground
⛳ PW (45°) — Most versatile. Go-to chip for most situations
🎯 GW (50°) — Softer landing, less run. Best from 15–25 yards or over light rough
💨 SW (54°) — Use when you need to carry a hazard or stop the ball fast
💬 More run needed → less loft. More carry needed → more loft. Simple.
Landing Spot Drill
Tee Target — Build Consistency
Place a tee 1.5 feet onto the green from the fringe edge. Forget the hole completely. Drop 10 balls and chip, trying to land each one on the tee. 6 out of 10 = consistent chipper. Once you hit 6/10, your chipping is predictable enough to score with.
💬 Pick a spot. Hit to the spot. Let the ball do the rest. Every chip should have a landing zone, not a hole target.

Gate Drill
Path & Face Training
Push 2 tees into the green 6 inches in front of the ball, slightly wider than your putter face. Roll the ball through the gate without touching either tee. Miss the tees = good path and face. Simple, brutal, effective.
💬 Do 10–15 reps from 4–5 feet. This is the single best putting drill you can do.
3-6-9 Challenge
Make the Shorts, Lag the Long
Place 3 balls at 3ft, 3 at 6ft, and 3 at 9ft from the hole. Goal: make all three 3-footers, 2-putt the 6-footers, and lag the 9-footers inside 2 feet. Count total putts out of 9. Target: 12 putts or fewer.
💬 3-footers are non-negotiable. Make them before you leave the putting green.
Distance Ladder
Speed Control — No Hole Needed
Putt 5 balls from 10 feet trying to stop within 1 foot of the fringe. Then 5 from 20 feet. Then 5 from 30 feet. No hole — pure speed control. This is where 3-putts are eliminated. The goal is never more than one foot of rollout past your target.
💬 Speed kills in putting. If every lag finishes within a foot, you'll never 3-putt.
Pre-Round Routine ⏱ 10 min
Do this every single time before a round. Not full swings — short game only. This dials in your touch for this specific green on this specific day.
🏌️ 5 putts to the fringe — no hole. Feel the speed of this green today, not your last round.
🎯 5 × 3-footers — build confidence, not mechanics. Make them all before moving on.
⛳ 3 chips from just off the green — feel the bump and run with GW, PW, and 7-iron once each.
✅ Always end on a make — walk to the first tee with the feeling of the ball going in the hole.
💬 This routine takes 10 minutes and can save you 3–5 strokes. Most amateurs skip it. Don't be most amateurs.

Tips to Make More 3-Footers
🎯 Commit to Your Line
Before every putt, crouch behind the ball and pick the exact line. Then step up and trust it — no second-guessing. The worst putts happen when you change your mind mid-stroke.
🎯 Keep Your Head Still
Put a tee in the ground where your ball was. After you putt, see if you can still see the tee — that means your head stayed still. If you can't find it, your head moved. Still head = consistent strike.

Dad Notes
Let her win enough that it's competitive. If she's crushing you, pretend to try harder. If she's frustrated, miss a few on purpose. The goal is she feels like a real golfer — because she is.
If she sinks one from distance: Completely lose your mind. Jump around. Tell her that's the best putt you've ever seen. She'll be talking about it for a week.

Your Alignment Putter
Your Lynx putter has built-in alignment lines — use them! Line up the stripe on your putter with your intended target line. When the lines match, you're aimed perfectly. Trust the tool and make a smooth stroke.
"Line it up, breathe out, roll it smooth."
The Putting Stroke 💜
1
Set Up Comfortably
  • Stand relaxed, feet about shoulder-width apart
  • Ball just inside your left foot
  • Eyes directly over the ball — lean forward gently if needed
2
Light, Relaxed Grip
  • Hold the putter very gently — light grip pressure is essential
  • Tension in the hands ruins feel
3
Pendulum Stroke
  • Rock your shoulders like a pendulum — back and through, even pace
  • No wrist flick needed — let the shoulders do the work
4
Hold the Finish
  • After you stroke it, hold the putter still for a moment
  • Watch — and enjoy — the ball rolling toward the hole 🌸
Putting Drills 🎯
🎯 Gate Drill
Place two tees about putter-head width apart, 6 inches in front of your ball. Practice rolling putts through the "gate." This promotes a straight, smooth stroke every time.
📏 3-Foot Confidence Builder
Place 6 balls in a circle about 3 feet from the hole. Putt each one in. When you make all 6, move to 4 feet. This is the most enjoyable drill in golf — and builds real confidence.
3 feet is where the game is won. Master this distance and watch your scores drop.
👁️ Eyes Closed Putting
Set up a 4-foot putt, then close your eyes and stroke it. This forces you to feel the pendulum motion instead of thinking about it. You'll be amazed how often it goes in — and how natural the stroke starts to feel.
Reading the Green 🌿
👀 Walk behind the ball — crouch and look at the slope between ball and hole
⛰️ Uphill putts — aim at the hole, stroke a little firmer
⬇️ Downhill putts — aim at the hole, use a very gentle stroke
↩️ Breaking putts — aim above the hole and let gravity curve it in
💜 Trust your read — commit and make a smooth stroke
