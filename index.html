The One Big Idea 🌺
Ball First, Ground Second
When you swing an iron, the club should touch the ball first — then brush the ground just after. If the club hits the ground before the ball, you'll hear a thud and the ball will barely move. That's the most common beginner mistake. Keep reminding yourself: ball first, ground second.
The ground comes second. Every time.

🏁 Phase 4 — Putting Game
Putting
Ring of Fire 🔥
Putter5 ballsPractice green
Place 5 balls in a circle around the hole, all exactly 3 feet away. You have to make ALL 5 in a row. Miss one? Start over. This is the same drill tour pros use every day.
💬 Pick a spot on the back of the cup and aim for that. Don't aim at the middle — aim for a specific point.
How to Make Every 3-Footer
🎯 Eyes Over the Ball
Stand over the ball and drop another ball from your nose — it should land on your ball. If it lands in front, you're too far away. If behind, too close. Eyes directly over ball = consistent path.
🎯 Pendulum Only
Shoulders rock, wrists stay locked. Imagine your arms and putter are one rigid triangle. Backswing and follow-through are the same length. No flipping the wrists at impact.
Kid's Golden Rules 🌟
Count your GOOD shots — forget the bad ones immediately.
Water break whenever you want. Seriously.
Stop when it stops being fun. Quality over quantity.
⏱ Keep sessions to 20–30 minutes max — short and focused beats long and tired.
One swing thought only: "TURN and FIRE!"

Before You Hit 🌸
Warm-Up First
Take 2–3 minutes to gently loosen up before reaching for a club. A few slow shoulder rolls, gentle hip circles, and easy torso turns are all you need. There's no rush — the range will wait for you.
"A warm body swings a happy club."
Phase 1 · Feel the Swing (6 balls)
7-Wood or Hybrid
Your most forgiving clubs. Start here every single time. No full swings yet — just easy half-swings to reconnect with the motion. Focus on the rhythm of the club moving back and through. If it feels good after 3, take a fuller swing on the last 3.
Half swing first Smooth tempo No power needed
Feel the clubhead swinging — let gravity do the work on the way down.
Phase 2 · Easy Irons (8 balls)
7-Iron or 8-Iron
Hit 4 balls with the 7-iron, then 4 with the 8-iron. These hollow-body irons are very forgiving — even off-center hits will fly nicely. One swing thought only: finish with your belt buckle facing the target.
Light grip Smooth finish Watch it fly 🌸
Swing to a full finish. If you finish tall, the swing was good.
Phase 3 · Short Game Finish (6 balls)
Pitching Wedge
End every session with short, gentle chip shots. Pick a spot about 20–30 yards away. Small, controlled swings — just enough to get the ball there softly. This is the most relaxing part of the session and builds great feel.
Tiny swing Land softly Breathe easy
"Small swing, big confidence." End on a great feeling.

Phase 1 · The 9-Iron (first 15 balls)
Start Here Every Time
The 9-iron is the shortest, easiest club to hit cleanly. Start every single session with it. No full swings yet — half-swings to find the rhythm, then grow into fuller swings. One thought only: ball first, ground second.
Half swing first Smooth tempo Hit the ball, not the ground

Phase 2 · Work up the Irons (10 balls)
8-iron → 6 → 5
Once the 9-iron is feeling good, step up slowly: a few 8-iron swings, then the 6, then the 5. The longer the club, the harder it is — that's normal. If anything stops feeling right, go back to the 9-iron and reset.
Same smooth tempo Don't swing harder Reset if it's off
Bigger club doesn't mean bigger swing. Let the club do the work.
Phase 3 · 3-Hybrid (5–10 balls)
Most Forgiving Long Club
The 3-hybrid looks longer but swings just like an iron. Ball slightly forward of center. Same smooth swing. It's much more forgiving than the 3-wood, so practice this one first — this is your long club.
Ball slightly forward Swing it like an iron Don't scoop
Phase 4 · 3-Wood (optional, 5 balls)
The Tricky One
The 3-wood is one of the hardest clubs for beginners — low expectations are totally fine. Ball forward (just inside the front heel). Sweep through, don't chop down. Don't try to lift it — the angled face does that for you.
Ball forward Sweep, don't chop Trust the loft
If it's not clicking, put it away and go back to the 3-hybrid. No shame.

End every session with short, gentle pitching-wedge shots — 20–30 yards, small controlled swings, soft landings. This is the most relaxing part of the session and it builds real feel. Walk off feeling good.

When Something Goes Wrong
💥 Thud, no distance: Ground came before the ball. Reset — ball first.
🪱 Ball rolled along the ground: You lifted your head early. Eyes stay down until after contact.
➡️ Ball went way right: Club face was open. Check grip — thumbs straight down the handle.
💨 Swung and missed: Totally normal. Slow down, eyes on the ball, try again.
🏌️ Wild, no control: Swung too hard. Take 20% off and stay smooth.
What to Ignore for Now 🌺
📏 Don't worry about distance — focus only on making contact.
🧠 Don't try to fix more than one thing per session.
🤷 A bad shot is not a big deal — every golfer hits them, even the pros.
🎯 Today's only goal: make contact. Everything else comes with time.

Before You Begin
All of these movements are gentle and optional. Do only what feels comfortable, and take a breath or short break between exercises whenever you need to. There is no wrong way to warm up — any movement is a win.
Shoulder & Neck
🌀 Slow Shoulder Rolls
Roll both shoulders forward 5 times, then backward 5 times. Slow and smooth. Feel the tension gently melt away.
Let gravity help — no need to force the movement.
↔️ Gentle Neck Turns
Slowly turn your head left, then right. Hold each side for 3 slow breaths. Just a gentle rotation — no strain needed.
Torso & Spine
🌿 Standing Torso Twist
Cross your arms over your chest. Gently rotate your upper body to the right, then to the left. Move only as far as feels comfortable — pause and breathe whenever you need to. This is the core of your golf swing motion.
This is your golf swing! Every gentle twist makes it feel more natural on the course.
🏌️ Slow Practice Swings
Without a ball, make 5 very slow, smooth practice swings with your 7-Wood or a mid-iron. Focus on how the swing feels — not how far the ball would go. This wakes up the right muscles gently.

Hold your arms in front of you and make slow circles with your wrists — 5 times each direction. Keeps the wrists loose and your grip relaxed.
🤝 Finger Flex & Release
Open your hands wide, then gently close them. Repeat 5 times. A simple way to warm up your grip before holding a club.
Hips & Balance
🔄 Gentle Hip Circles
Stand with feet shoulder-width apart. Make slow, gentle circles with your hips — 5 each direction. Rest between sets if needed. This helps free up the lower body for a smooth turn.
Hold a club or chair for balance if you'd like — take your time with every movement.

Mobility · Flow · Stretch  ·  2–4x per week  ·  25–35 min  ·  Dumbbells + TRX optional

⚠️ Knee-safe throughout. Always use padding under knees when kneeling. Never push through pain.

🌊 Flow Sequence — 10 min Move slowly. Breathe into each position. Link the movements.

Standing Thoracic Rotation — 10 slow reps each side

Club or broomstick across shoulders, soft knees in golf posture. Rotate fully and hold 2–3 sec at end range. Breathe out as you turn. This IS your backswing and follow-through — own it. ✅ Knee safe.

Standing Hip Circle — 2 × 10 each direction each leg

Hold wall or TRX handle for light balance support. Lift one knee to hip height and draw slow, controlled circles. Standing knee soft. The most golf-specific move in the routine — trains hip mobility in the exact range you need at impact. ✅ Knee safe.

Thread the Needle — 60 sec each side

On all fours (pad under knees), slide one arm under your body and rotate the upper spine toward the floor. Breathe deeply and let the rotation deepen on each exhale. Opens thoracic spine — directly increases shoulder turn depth. ✅ Knee safe.

Cat-Cow — 8 slow reps

On all fours, exhale and arch the back up (cat), inhale and drop the belly down (cow). Slow and fluid — match movement to breath. Wakes up the entire spine and sets a calm rhythm for the session. ✅ Knee safe.

Glute Bridge — 3 × 12 slow reps

Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat. Drive hips up squeezing glutes, hold 2 sec at the top, lower slowly. Feel every rep — this isn't a grind, it's a deliberate activation. Weak glutes = swaying hips = missed shots. ✅ Knee safe.

🧘 Deep Stretches — 12–15 min Hold longer than feels necessary. Breathe into tight spots.

90/90 Hip Stretch — 90 sec each side

Sit on floor, both legs at 90°. Sit tall, breathe deep, let the hips open gradually. The single best hip opener for golf — tight hips = blocked rotation = weak swing. ✅ Knee safe.

Child's Pose with Side Reach — 45 sec each side

From kneeling (pillow under knees), sit hips back toward heels and reach arms long to one side. Breathe into the stretch along your side body. Opens lats — tight lats = over-the-top swing. ✅ Knee safe — use padding.

Half-Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch — 60 sec each side

Folded towel under back knee. Torso tall, gentle forward shift of the hips. Breathe slowly. Tight hip flexors = early extension = weak impact. ✅ Knee safe — always use padding.

Supine Spinal Twist — 45 sec each side

Lie on back, one knee crosses body to opposite side. Arm out, gaze the other way. Let gravity do the work — no forcing. Decompresses the spine and restores thoracic rotation. Great to end with. ✅ Knee safe.

Figure-4 Glute Stretch — 60 sec each side

Lie on back, cross ankle over opposite knee, gently pull uncrossed leg toward chest. Deep glute and hip opener. Breathe slowly. Tight glutes block hip rotation through impact. ✅ Knee safe — lying down, no load.

Chest Opener — 30 sec

TRX handles or doorframe — face away, one handle in each hand, arms back and slightly upward. Walk feet toward the anchor to deepen. Long slow breaths. Counters daily forward posture for a fuller shoulder turn. ✅ Knee safe.

⚡ Movement — 8–10 min (optional) Light and smooth. Golf-specific patterns only.

TRX Rotational Row — 3 × 10 each side

Handles at shoulder height, stand sideways. Grip with both hands, lean back slightly, pull to chest while rotating torso away from the anchor. Slow and controlled — feel the rotation. Handles in hands only. ✅ Knee safe.

TRX Fallout — 3 × 8 reps

Face the anchor, arms extended forward at shoulder height, body angled back. Let arms travel up and overhead in a long smooth arc, keeping the body in a straight line. Handles in hands — feet on the floor. Trains the stretch-load of the backswing. ✅ Knee safe.

Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift — 3 × 10 reps

Soft knees only — no deep bend. Hinge from hips, dumbbells close to shins. Drive hips forward to stand. Slow on the way down. Same hip-hinge pattern as your downswing. Start light. ✅ Knee safe.

Dumbbell Lateral Raise — 2 × 12 reps

Light weight, arms to shoulder height only. Slow and controlled — no momentum. Shoulder stability for a consistent swing plane. ✅ Knee safe.

Chipping 🏌️
Bump & Run Setup
The Most Reliable Chip in the Bag
Ball back in stance 70% weight lead Hands forward
Ball positioned toward your trail foot. Weight 70% on lead (right) foot at setup and through impact. Shaft leans toward target — hands ahead of the ball. This delofts the club and keeps the trajectory low and rolling.
💬 Pendulum stroke — no wrist flip. Think long putt with a lofted club. Follow-through matches backswing length.
Club Selection Guide
🏒 7-Iron — Lots of run. Best from 5+ yards off the fringe on flat ground
⛳ PW (45°) — Most versatile. Go-to chip for most situations
🎯 GW (50°) — Softer landing, less run. Best from 15–25 yards or over light rough
💨 SW (54°) — Use when you need to carry a hazard or stop the ball fast
💬 More run needed → less loft. More carry needed → more loft. Simple.
Landing Spot Drill
Tee Target — Build Consistency
Place a tee 1.5 feet onto the green from the fringe edge. Forget the hole completely. Drop 10 balls and chip, trying to land each one on the tee. 6 out of 10 = consistent chipper. Once you hit 6/10, your chipping is predictable enough to score with.
💬 Pick a spot. Hit to the spot. Let the ball do the rest. Every chip should have a landing zone, not a hole target.
Putting 🎯
Setup Fundamentals
👁️ Eyes over ball — lean forward until eyes are directly above the ball
🤲 Light grip — 4 out of 10 — tension kills feel. Barely there.
⚖️ Pendulum stroke — shoulders rock, wrists stay still
↔️ Follow-through = backswing length — even tempo both ways
👂 Listen for the rattle — don't watch the ball, look up after you hear it
Gate Drill
Path & Face Training
Push 2 tees into the green 6 inches in front of the ball, slightly wider than your putter face. Roll the ball through the gate without touching either tee. Miss the tees = good path and face. Simple, brutal, effective.
💬 Do 10–15 reps from 4–5 feet. This is the single best putting drill you can do.
3-6-9 Challenge
Make the Shorts, Lag the Long
Place 3 balls at 3ft, 3 at 6ft, and 3 at 9ft from the hole. Goal: make all three 3-footers, 2-putt the 6-footers, and lag the 9-footers inside 2 feet. Count total putts out of 9. Target: 12 putts or fewer.
💬 3-footers are non-negotiable. Make them before you leave the putting green.
Distance Ladder
Speed Control — No Hole Needed
Putt 5 balls from 10 feet trying to stop within 1 foot of the fringe. Then 5 from 20 feet. Then 5 from 30 feet. No hole — pure speed control. This is where 3-putts are eliminated. The goal is never more than one foot of rollout past your target.
💬 Speed kills in putting. If every lag finishes within a foot, you'll never 3-putt.
Pre-Round Routine ⏱ 10 min
Do this every single time before a round. Not full swings — short game only. This dials in your touch for this specific green on this specific day.
🏌️ 5 putts to the fringe — no hole. Feel the speed of this green today, not your last round.
🎯 5 × 3-footers — build confidence, not mechanics. Make them all before moving on.
⛳ 3 chips from just off the green — feel the bump and run with GW, PW, and 7-iron once each.
✅ Always end on a make — walk to the first tee with the feeling of the ball going in the hole.
💬 This routine takes 10 minutes and can save you 3–5 strokes. Most amateurs skip it. Don't be most amateurs.
